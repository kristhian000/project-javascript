Challenges and Risks of AI

Ethics

Biases: Models can perpetuate inequalities if the data used contains biases.

Privacy: Massive data collection raises questions about the proper use of personal information.

Impact on Employment

Automation is replacing repetitive jobs, but it also creates new roles related to the supervision and development of AI.

Security

Deepfakes: The creation of fake content poses a risk to public trust.

Cybersecurity: Hackers can use AI to carry out more sophisticated attacks.

Regulation

It is crucial to establish clear laws that control the ethical use of AI without stifling innovation.

Fun Facts

Alan Turing proposed a test in 1950 to determine whether a machine can imitate human behavior to the point of fooling an evaluator.

In the 1940s, computers were nicknamed "electronic brains," long before AI as we know it today was even thought of.

The first game to use AI was Draughts, developed by Arthur Samuel in 1952.

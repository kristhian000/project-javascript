Artificial Intelligence

AI algorithms and models

Machine Learning (ML)

Machine learning is a subcategory of AI that enables machines to automatically improve based on experience.

  • Supervised learning: Uses labeled data to train models. Example: predicting the price of a house based on historical data.
  • Unsupervised learning: Finding patterns in unlabeled data. Example: clustering customers by purchasing behavior.
  • Reinforcement learning: Training agents that make decisions in an environment to maximize a cumulative reward. Example: robots playing video games or autonomous control systems.

Deep Learning:

NLP enables machines to interpret, understand and respond to human language.

  • Example: Chatbots like GPT that answer questions or generate coherent text.
  • Technologies like transformers have revolutionized the field, allowing the creation of models like GPT and BERT.

Other Algorithms:

  • Decision trees: Used to classify data or make decisions based on criteria.
  • SVM (Support Vector Machines): Finding patterns in unlabeled data. Effective in classification and regression.
  • K-means Popular clustering algorithm in unsupervised learning.

Fun Facts

Biologically inspired algorithms: Genetic algorithms mimic natural evolution, using concepts like mutation and selection to solve problems.

"Sleeping" neural networks: Although the concept was invented in the 1950s, they were not widely used until modern hardware allowed their implementation.

The Perceptron: It was one of the first models of neural networks, created in 1958, but it was very limited in solving complex problems.
